Rita Ora talks life in Australia and what she really thinks about her fellow The Voice coaches

Rita Ora is back and ready to take on The Voice Australia for the second year running.

Georgie Kearney / The Voice Australia / Updated 3 hours ago
Share

Reality TV personalities Kim and Khloe Kardashian and Kris and Kylie Jenner found themselves in a decidedly different environment on Monday, sitting in a Los Angeles courtroom as jury selection began ahead of a defamation lawsuit filed against them by model Blac Chyna.

As the process unfolded, the group listened to comments from prospective jurors about the parties, which ranged from nice to not so nice, as attorneys struggled to find panellists with an open mind.

" I hope none of these people get any wealthier because of this, for sure, " a Long Beach man and current computer science student said.

A female juror echoed his remarks, saying, " It’s more money for ... their pockets and less for us, " she said.

" In the end they shouldn’t be getting any money, " another female juror said.

Yet another woman panellist said she did not believe the Kardashians were good role models for her daughters.

" I’m not sure I would be fair to them, " the woman said.

Others criticised the concept of reality television in general, the impact of social media and what they perceived as the Kardashians’ sense of entitlement.

But another female juror said she did not feel she could be fair to Chyna, because she is a longtime fan of the Kardashians and their show.

" I’m a pretty good fan of the defendants, so I don’t think I can be unbiased, " said the hair stylist from Koreatown.

Another female juror said she had learned about Chyna’s case against the Kardashians and Jenners on TikTok and would probably lean toward the side of the defendants.

" I don’t want to waste you guys’ time, " she said.

One male juror said he has seen the 2002 sex tape between Kim Kardashian and Ray J and believed it would disrupt his concentration in listening to the trial evidence.

" I’m going to be replaying this sex tape in my head and am probably not going to be paying attention very closely, " said the juror, whose comments were cut off by Judge Gregory W Alarcon, who said, " I think we get the point. "

The Kardashians and Jenners showed no reaction to the jurors’ statements.

Chyna’s real name is Angela White.

She claims the four socialites made false statements about her, causing her to lose considerable money from the cancellation of the Rob & Chyna show, a reality series the model appeared in with former fiancé Rob Kardashian.

The lawsuit alleges defamation and interference with prospective economic advantage.

Chyna alleges the four women falsely claimed she physically abused Rob while they were a couple.
